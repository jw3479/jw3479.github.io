Jia Wan

I am currently at the University of Oxford studying statistics and AI on a Rhodes Scholarship. I am doing my master's with the FLAIR lab where I work on zero-shot coordination problems in multi-agent reinforcement learning with Jakob Foerster. I am also a non-residential research fellow with the Regulation, Evaluation, and Governance Lab (RegLab) at Stanford Law School working with Daniel Ho and Jacob Goldin on algorithmic fairness.

Prior to Oxford, I studied mathematics and computer science at Columbia University, where I was fortunate to work with Clifford Stein and Yuri Faenza on matching theory.

In my spare time I like (amateurly) writing music with piano, guitar, drums or MIDI keyboard, performing standup comedy, long-distance running, and skateboarding.

You can reach me at jia [dot] wan [AT] brasenose [dot] ox [dot] ac [dot] uk.
