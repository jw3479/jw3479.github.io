Jia Wan

I am a first year PhD student in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology. I am fortunate to be advised by Prof. Devavrat Shah and Prof. Martin Wainwright. I was previously a Rhodes scholar at the University of Oxford studying statistics and worked with Prof. Jakob Foerster on zero-shot coordination problems in multi-agent reinforcement learning. I was also a non-residential research fellow at the Regulation, Evaluation, and Governance Lab (RegLab) at Stanford Law School working with Prof. Daniel Ho and Prof. Jacob Goldin on algorithmic fairness.

Prior to Oxford, I studied mathematics and computer science at Columbia University, where I was fortunate to work with Prof. Yuri Faenza and Prof. Clifford Stein on the intersection of mechanism design and theoretical computer science (focusing on matching theory).

In my spare time I like (amateurly) composing / producing music with piano, guitar and MIDI, long-distance running, gymming, stand-up comedy, and skateboarding.

You can reach me at jiawan [AT] mit [dot] edu.
